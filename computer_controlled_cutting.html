Computer Controlled Cutting

Vinyl Cutter

In this section, I used Affinity Designer to create a vinyl sticker just for personal use. I made the Batman logo just becasue Batman is one of my favorite superheroes.

Step-by-Step Process

Below is a detailed step-by-step process of how I created the vinyl sticker. Each step includes screenshots and explanations.

Step 1: Outline

I started by importing an outline of the Batman symbol so that I can trace it to make the sticker look as accurate as possible.

Step 2: Trace

This was my sketch of the bat. I traced it, but there were some spots that didn't come out perfect. I decided to keep the imperfections and just continue on.

Step 3: Fill

Here, I filled in the bat to make it a solid black.

Step 4: Black Oval

Next, I made the bottom layer. I made a black oval as the bottom part of the sticker.

Step 5: Yellow Oval

I then made the second layer, which was a smaller yellow oval.

Step 6: Background

Here I put the yellow oval over the balck oval, creating the background of the sticker where the bat will be.

Step 7: Final Sticker

Finally, I put the bat into place, creating the final product.

Step 8: Final Cut Out

Here is my vinyl sticker after it was cut out.

Laser Cutter

In this section, I used Fusion 360 and Affinity Designer to design a three piece puzzle that would be laser cut. I decided to make a bird with the three pieces being the main body, the tail, and the feet.

Step-by-Step Process

Below is a detailed step-by-step documentation of how I designed the puzzle. Each step includes screenshots and explanations.

Step 1: Head

I began by sketching the bird's head and beak.

Step 2: Torso

Next, I created a torso which attached to the head. I added slits on the back and bottom where the other puzzle pieces would fit in.

Step 3: Tail

Here I sketched a tail that would interlock with the back of the torso.

Step 4: Feet

Then, I made the feet of the bird that would connect to the bottom of the torso.

Step 5: Top View

This is a top view of all of my sketches.

Step 6: Isometric View

This is an isometric view of all the finish puzzle pieces.

Step 7: Imported into Affinity Designer

In this step, I imported the sketches into Affinity Designer. Here, I can save them as a pdf so that I can send them to be laser cut.

Step 8: Final Cut Out

Here is my puzzle after it was cut out. It ended up being a little bit messed up so I have to redesign and cut it out.
