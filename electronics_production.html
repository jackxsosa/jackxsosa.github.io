Electronics Production

Project Overview

In this project, I used KiCAD to design a circuit that takes a power source and runs an output component, such as an LED or motor. This documentation tracks my design process with step-by-step explanations and images of each stage.

Step-by-Step Process

Step 1: Setting Up KiCAD and Choosing Components

In this step, I opened KiCAD and selected the necessary components for the circuit: a power source, an output component (LED, motor, etc.), and connecting elements.

Step 2: Creating the Schematic

In this step, I designed the schematic for the circuit by connecting the power source to the output component. I used KiCAD's schematic editor to arrange the components and ensure correct connections.

Step 3: Routing the PCB

After completing the schematic, I switched to the PCB layout editor in KiCAD to place the components on a PCB and route the connections. I took into account the size and layout for optimal performance.

Step 4: Finalizing the Design and Preparing for Fabrication

In this final step, I reviewed the design to ensure all connections were correct. I then generated the Gerber files for fabrication, which would be sent to a PCB manufacturer if produced physically.
