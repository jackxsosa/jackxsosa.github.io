Solution Design

Project Requirements Overview

Below are the 10 project requirements that I will fulfill as part of my final project.

Requirement 1: Prototype Development

Create a small-scale prototype to validate the design concept.

Requirement 2: Scalability

Scale the prototype to incorporate a "Growing Beyond Earth" LED panel.

Requirement 3: Zero Gravity Simulation

Ensure the system can simulate zero gravity by rotating plants on multiple axes.

Requirement 4: Light Integration

Integrate LED panel to provide adjustable light that moves with the plants.

Requirement 5: Watering System

Develop a system for watering the plants while they are in motion.

Requirement 6: Random Motion

Ensure that the movement of the clinostat is random to create a better low-gravity simulation.

Requirement 7: Plant Encasement and Diversity

Create or utilize a casing that will sit in the middle of the clinostat without moving and allow for a wide variety of vegetation to be able to grow in it.

Requirement 8: Lightweight and Durable

Construct the clinostat out of lightweight and durable material to ensure that there is limited stress across the entire device.

Requirement 9: Testing and Validation

Perform a series of tests to assess the performance of the scaled-up clinostat, and record/analyze the growth of plants under various conditions and compare with control samples.

Requirement 10: Cost Efficient

Establish a design for the clinostat that is cost-effective, ensuring that several clinostats can be constructed at as cheap a price as possible if more are needed.

1. Problem Definition

  • [ ] Clear problem statement
  • [ ] Statement of purpose
  • [ ] Identification of stakeholders (consumers, end-users, experts)

2. Concept Development

  • [ ] Generate multiple solution ideas (at least one per group member)
  • Document each concept with:
    • [ ] Concept number and team member responsible
    • [ ] Description of concept
    • [ ] Additional research on the concept
    • [ ] Functionality and operation explanation
    • [ ] Justification for why it's a strong solution
    • [ ] Annotated sketches and drawings
    • [ ] (For physical products) Preliminary ideas about size, shape, materials, and other features

3. Concept Analysis

  • [ ] Create a SINGLE POWERPOINT SLIDE for the top 2 ideas
  • [ ] Include problem statement and statement of purpose on display
  • [ ] Gather feedback from qualified individuals (consumers, stakeholders, experts)

4. Solution Selection

  • [ ] Use decision matrix to compare top concepts
  • [ ] Refine concepts based on feedback and decision matrix results
  • [ ] Create new or revised product concepts (at least 1)
  • [ ] Select best solution concept
  • Create final concept selection documentation including:
    • [ ] Title page
    • [ ] Detailed problem statement
    • [ ] Product description
    • [ ] Operation explanation
    • [ ] Justification for selection
    • [ ] Appendix with design specifications, brainstorming ideas, product concepts, and consumer feedback

5. Detailed Design

  • [ ] Create detailed CAD working drawings, annotated sketches, or pseudocode
  • [ ] Include dimensioned part drawings and assembly drawings (if applicable)
  • Assess design for improvement factors:
    • [ ] Function
    • [ ] Aesthetics
    • [ ] Ergonomics
    • [ ] Safety and Ethics
    • [ ] Cost
    • [ ] Standardization
    • [ ] Quality Control
    • [ ] Ease of Maintenance
    • [ ] Durability
    • [ ] Environmental impact
  • [ ] Conduct peer review of all documentation
  • [ ] Implement agreed-upon improvements

6. Cost Estimation

  • [ ] Estimate cost for prototype production
  • [ ] Research and document cost of each component
  • [ ] Estimate labor time and cost
  • [ ] Calculate total prototype production cost (materials + labor)

7. Presentation and Communication (Preparation of PDR)

  • [ ] Prepare for concept presentation to reviewers
  • [ ] Practice professional communication with stakeholders and experts
  • [ ] Be prepared to explain and justify design decisions
